No Veteran ever pays for COMMIT services.

  • Currently working with 500 servicemembers and veterans in 2018, COMMIT strives to work with another 600 servicemembers and veterans in 2019 through One-to-One Transitioning Assistance.

  • Impacting over 300 servicemembers and veterans and 150 corporate mentors, COMMIT will conduct 20 Transition Mentoring Workshops in 2018 and 2019

  • In July, 2018 COMMIT launched the Digital Library impacting exponentially more service members at domestic and overseas military bases, as they are considering their first steps out of uniform.

  • COMMIT launched Veteran Transition Coaching software in March 2018, and plans to bring 450 persons through vTc by the end of 2019. The technology enables us to better track, monitor, and report results while significantly scaling the number of servicemembers and veterans served.

Why Commit?

Renew Perspective

COMMIT believes you can change someone’s perspective and thus their trajectory in a few minutes of quality dialogue.

Quality Over Quantity

I’m having difficulty broadening the aperture to rethink and redefine what is possible.

Invest In Those With The Greatest Potential

COMMIT believes we do a disservice to our Nation by not investing in those that add the most value.

Change The Narrative And Challenge Assumptions

COMMIT recognizes high value servicemembers and veterans are often overlooked from a services standpoint because people think they don’t need help.
